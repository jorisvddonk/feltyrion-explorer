P06, FELISIAN WORLD NUMBER 4 OF 5, PRO 0.306.000; PRV 0.329.309.856
 /// A BIT LIKE SOLDESCO (P05) IN APPEARANCE WITH SLIGHTLY LARGER
BODIES OF WATER, BUT MANY LANDING SITES WERE QUITE FILLED WITH
VEGETATION. PROBABLY BECAUSE THERE ALWAYS SEEMS TO BE MASSIVE
STORM AREAS. OF NOTE IS THAT SOME FEATURES ARE INDISTINCT DUE TO
VEGETATION IN DIRECT LIGHT, AND ARE MORE DETAILED IN SUBDUED LIGHT
(LIKE IN THE RAIN) /// ON THIS WORLD TWO PYRAMIDS WERE FOUND. ONE
AT 181.060, -37.27, AND THE OTHER HALF A WORLD AWAY AT 359.060,
24.-09 /// AT 179.081, A VEGETATION COVERED, FACETED AND WIDE BASED
PEAK (OR VERY TALL BOULDER?) IS A QUITE VISIBLE FEATURE. /// AT
356.002, -74.19, IS A PEAK SIMILAR TO 179.081, BUT IN A POLAR
CLIMATE. THIS ONE APPEARS TO BE STONE COVERED WITH ICE. /// AT
336.101, ALSO A POLAR CLIMATE, APPEARS AN INTERESTING GROUP OF
THREE SEPERATE LARGE BOULDER OBJECTS IN A CLOSE VICINITY. ONE ON
NORMAL GROUND, ANOTHER ON THE SHORE, AND THE THIRD FAR OFF INTO THE
 ICE. THE FIRST AT -35.-55, THE SECOND AT -60.52, AND THE THIRD IS
 PAST THE EXPLORATION LIMIT OF -71.-59. /// AT 005.056, -07.13, IS
AN UNUSUALLY SHAPED PEAK WHICH DUE TO TAPERING AND ANGLED FACETS,
HAS THE NORTHWESTERLY FACE APPEARING TO HAVE A ROUNDED TOP, BUT AS
ONE LOOKS AT ITS SOUTHERLY FACE IT HAS AN EXTREME POINT.
A DEPRESSION IS ON THE NORTH SIDE. AT 345.066, 15.36, IS A SHARP
 POINTED PEAK SIMILAR TO 005.056, BUT IN THIS CASE IT IS A MASSIVE
BASE WITH A PORTION OF THE SOUTHERLY SIDE HAVING THE EXTREME POINT
EXTENDING FROM IT. HAS A SHALLOW TRENCH AT GROUND LEVEL ON THE
SOUTHERLY TO SOUTHWESTERLY PORTION. /// AT 031.076 IS ANOTHER PEAK
WITH CHARACTERISTICS OF BOTH 005.056 AND 345.066, BUT WITH A MORE
LENGTHY AND ANGULAR SLOPING PORTION BEHIND THE MAIN PEAK, AND A
BOULDER ATTACHED TO THE END. /// AT 215.114 IS A CLUSTER OF VARIOUS
 LITHIC PORTIONS OF NO GREAT HEIGHT INVOLVED WITH EACH OTHER NEAR A
 VERY LARGE POND. THIS FEATURE IS MADE ALL THE MORE OBSERVABLE DUE
 TO THE SURROUNDING ALMOST FLAT GROUND OF THE SECTOR. /// AT
032.076, 22.17, WHAT WAS THOUGHT TO BE A MESA-TYPE LITHIC TURNED
INTO A GROUPING OF PEAKS OF A VARIETY OF SIZES CLUSTERED IN ONE
AREA, MOSTLY COJOINED AT THEIR BASES, THOUGH THE SOUTHERLY END
FORMS INTO SPLIT UPPER PORTION SMALL MESA. A FAIRLY LARGE WATER
FILLED PIT IS ON THE EASTERLY SIDE OF THE FEATURE. /// THE AREA
SURROUNDING 034.076 APPEARED FROM ORBIT TO BE UNUSUAL- LIKE AN OLD
CRATER ZONE. INVESTIGATION OF SECTORS PROVIDED 031.076 AND 032.076
, AND FINALLY AT 033.078, 06.42, WAS FOUND A LITHIC MASS SIMILAR TO
 215.114. DOMINATED BY A MAIN SHORT PEAK, THE LITHIC CLUSTER FORMS
