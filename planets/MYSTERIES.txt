Planet: MYSTERIES
Starmap index: 40
Paris coordinates: 5069264, 20652333, 117733

Guide entries:
A VERY INTERESTING PLANET. UPON FIRST LANDING, IT APPEARED TO BE
ENTIRELY FROZEN, BUT SUBSEQUENT LANDINGS UNCOVERED EXTREMELY
INTERESTING FINDINGS.
 SECTOR 212:1 - I HAVE FOUND A LARGE STRETCH OF FROZEN OCEAN, WITH
AN ARCHIPELAGO OF AT LEAST 100 SMALL ISLANDS STICKING OUT OF THE ICY
SURFACE. NO LIFE WAS FOUND.
SECTOR 145:-50 - I HAVE FOUND A LUSH, BOGGY AREA. THE TEMPERATURE IS
ABOVE 0, MUCH HIGHER THAN THE -120 I HAVE FOUND EARLIER. THE LAND IS
 COVERED WITH SOME FORM OF GRASS, AND THERE ARE MANY SMALL LAKES AND
PONDS ALL AROUND. GROWING AROUND, AND SOMETIMES INSIDE THESE PONDS
ARE FASCINATING, PINK BUSHES.
SECTOR 264:3 - I HAVE FOUND A SMALL ISLAND OVERGROWN BY GRASS IN THE
 MIDDLE OF A LARG BODY OF WATER. NOT SEEING ANY SHORES, I TAKE IT TO
BE A SEA. WHILE MY EARLIER FIND WAS A FROZEN SEA, THIS ONE WAS IN
LIQUID FORM. THE MOST MYSTERIOUS THING WAS AN UNEXPLICABLE WEATHER
PHENOMENON. EVERY MINUTE OR SO, A WAVE OF FAST-MOVING (FOG?),
(STORM?), (WIND?) APPEARED FROM THE SOUTH. IT HAD SMALL LIGHTS
PLAYING ON ITS SURFACE, AND MY VISION DIMMED WHEN IT WASHES OVER ME,
 BUFFETING ME AROUND WITH HIGH FORCE.
(NOTES BY FARAGO, 6011.582.720)
ALSO, ON ONE LANDING, I WAS GREETED BY NOT LESS THAN FOUR BIRDS AND
TWO, MAYBE THREE QUADRUPEDS IN MY IMMEDIATE VICINITY. BI HAVE NEVER
OBSERVED SUCH A HIGH DENSITY OF ANIMALS BEFORE. (NOTE BY FARAGO)
