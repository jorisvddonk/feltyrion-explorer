Guide entries:
A FELYSIAN PLANET, PART OF THE GLIESEN SYSTEM. IT'S NICE TO SEE THAT
LIFE OUT HERE, TENS OF THOUSANDS OF LIGHT YEARS FROM FELYSIA.
GRAVITY HERE IS GOOD, PRESSURE A LITTLE LOW, AND THE AVERAGE TEMP IS
ALSO SLIGHTLY ON THE COOL SIDE. THE SURFACE IS ABOUT 40% WATER, AND IS
FOUND IN SMALL SEAS AND POCKETS INSTEAD OF LARGE OCEANS.
3.24:GREEN SLOPING MEADOWS.
334.7:A FROZEN ISLAND NEAR THE NORTH POLE. PRETTY WARM FOR THAT AREA
OF A FELYSIAN PLANET, AT ABOUT -28 DEGREES. THE SNOW APPEARS PURPLE,
BUT THAT MIGHT JUST BE THE LIGHT. THERE IS EVIDENCE OF GLACIAL ACTIVITY
IN THE AREA, JUDGING BY THE VALLEYS CARVED OUT.
357.60:LANDING SITE IS ON SLOPE OF CANYON CONTAINING A LAKE
330.60:GROUND LOOKS GREEN AND FROZEN
